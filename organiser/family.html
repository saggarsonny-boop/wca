Children's Guidance Resources

Age-appropriate guidelines to help children understand the separation process.

Ages 3-5: "Mommy/Daddy is away helping people" Use simple terms. Avoid complex judicial concepts. Reassure them that they are safe, loved, and that the parent will return.
Ages 6-9: "Why is Daddy/Mommy away?" Explain that rules exist for everyone, and sometimes grown-ups have to take a time-out to resolve rule issues. Emphasize it is not their fault.
Ages 10-12: "The justice system and what it means" Introduce basic legal rules. Discuss how courts decide terms, and answer their direct questions honestly without burdening them with worry.
Ages 13-15: "Understanding the legal process" Engage in more mature discussions about rehabilitation, compliance, and guidelines. Keep lines of communication open.
Ages 16-18: "Preparing for reentry" Discuss reentry preparation, support mechanisms, and how the family unit will collaborate to build a stable transition pathway.
