📋 PSR Pre-Sentence Interview Intake Simulator

Draft your responses to the 5 critical sections of the probation officer's interview. Your drafts are saved securely and can be reviewed with your defense counsel.

Legal Notice & Disclaimer: The suggestions, rules, and rules of thumb displayed below are general informational guides drawn from public guidelines. They do NOT constitute legal advice or represent a professional legal consultation. You must review all your draft responses with your defense attorney before sharing them with your probation officer or the court.
Rule: Express remorse and take responsibility. Do not blame co-defendants, minimize your actions, or complain about investigators. Acceptance of responsibility grants you a 3-level guideline reduction.
* What to write: "I deeply regret my actions. I recognize that I caused harm to [victims/family]..."
* What to avoid: "I didn't know it was illegal" or "I only did it because my boss told me to."
Rule: Detail family stability, marriages, child care obligations, and sick relatives. The BOP uses this to place you at a facility within 500 miles of your family.
* What to write: List children, spouses, parents you care for, and their dependence on your presence/income.
Rule: Disclose all physical illnesses, learning disabilities, anxiety, depression, and medications. This maps you to a correct BOP Medical Care Level (Tier 1-4) and guarantees prescriptions.
* What to write: Chronic conditions (asthma, diabetes, high blood pressure), past therapy/counseling, and current prescriptions.
Rule: You must document a history of drug or alcohol abuse in the 12 months prior to arrest to qualify for the 12-month RDAP sentence reduction. Disclose it here honestly.
* What to write: Frequency of use, age of first use, impact on work/relationships, and past rehab/counseling.
* Crucial: If it is not documented in the PSR, the BOP will disqualify you from the program.
Rule: Detail all assets, debts, cash, and expenses. The probation officer sets your fine payment schedules based on this. Concealing assets is a crime and blocks acceptance points.
* What to write: Home equity, bank accounts, monthly living expenses (rent, groceries), child support, and outstanding loans.
