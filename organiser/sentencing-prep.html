📋 PSR Pre-Sentence Interview Guide

The Pre-Sentence Report (PSR) is the single most important document determining your custody conditions and programs. Prepare for the interview using these guidelines:

The Guideline: Express genuine remorse for your actions. Do not minimize your role, blame co-conspirators, or complain about prosecuting agencies. The probation officer tracks acceptance of responsibility to justify your 3-level guideline reduction.

The Guideline: Detail your family support structure, children, and marriages. If you have close family ties, this justifies placement recommendations near your home municipality (the BOP's 500-mile rule).

The Guideline: Document any mental health history, anxiety, depression, or physical conditions. If you have documented diagnoses, it helps secure placement at facilities with medical capabilities (BOP Care Levels 2-4) and access to counseling.

The Guideline: If you have a history of drug or alcohol abuse, you must disclose it honestly. Documenting this history in the PSR is **mandatory** to qualify for the Residential Drug Abuse Program (RDAP), which can reduce your sentence by up to 12 months.

The Guideline: Provide a complete, honest ledger of assets and liabilities. The probation officer uses this to calculate fine ranges and set restitution payment plans. Concealing assets will block your acceptance of responsibility points.
