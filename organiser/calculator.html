How These Numbers Are Calculated ("The Working")

1. Good Conduct Time (GCT): By federal law, inmates receive up to 54 days of good time credit per year of sentence imposed (~14.79% reduction). This is applied from day 1 for any sentence greater than 12 months.

2. Prior Jail / Remand Credit: Any time spent locked up in county jail or non-BOP custody before sentencing/surrender is credited day-for-day off your prison sentence. However, this period does NOT qualify for First Step Act programming credits because program credits are only earned while in a designated BOP facility.

3. RDAP drug program reduction: Completing the Residential Drug Abuse Program (RDAP) grants a sentence reduction of up to 12 months. The reduction scale is: • Sentence of 37+ months: 12 months off.
• Sentence of 31–36 months: 9 months off.
• Sentence of 24–30 months: 6 months off.
• Sentence under 24 months: Not eligible for sentence reduction.

4. First Step Act (FSA) Credits: Eligible inmates earn 10 to 15 days of early release credit for every 30 days of productive programming. • Up to 365 days (12 months) of earned credits are applied directly to advance your release date from prison.
• Any excess credits earned beyond 12 months are applied to speed up your transfer to home confinement or a halfway house.
• Note: The first 6 months of an inmate's prison term are when assessments are conducted, programs are assigned, and credits accumulate; early release credits cannot be formally applied to advance release dates until they are fully earned and documented.

* Calculations are estimates based on standard BOP policies: Good Conduct Time is calculated at 54 days per year (approx 14.8%). RDAP reduction depends on sentence length (12 months off for 37+ months, 9 months for 31-36 months, 6 months for 24-30 months). First Step Act credits assume maximum program completion (15 days earned per month, capped at 1 year early release, with excess applied to home confinement). Actual outcomes are decided entirely by the Bureau of Prisons and probation officers.
