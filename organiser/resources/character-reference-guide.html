Structuring the Character Reference Letter Portfolio

A complete guide on who to ask, what to include, and how to format reference submissions.

1. Who to Ask

Select individuals who know you well and can speak to your character, work ethic, and values. Ideally, source letters from:

  • Professional colleagues and former employers
  • Community leaders or charity coordinators
  • Family members and lifelong friends

2. What to Include

Each letter should be addressed to the sentencing judge and follow this structure:

  • Introduction: State how long and in what capacity the author has known you.
  • Knowledge of Offense: Clarify that the author is aware of the charges you face.
  • Observations: Focus on specific traits like remorse, community contributions, and family devotion.

3. Sample Letter Outline

Dear Judge [Name],

I am writing this letter in support of [Defendant Name]...
I have known them for over [Number] years in my capacity as...
I am aware that they have pleaded guilty to [Offense]...
Throughout our association, I have observed them to be...

Sincerely,
[Author Name]
