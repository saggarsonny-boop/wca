Federal Justice Reference Data

The federal criminal justice system uses quantitative point thresholds to determine sentencing ranges, custody levels, and supervision guidelines.

Federal Sentencing Zones & Sentencing Options

Zone Guideline Range (Months) Sentencing Options & Requirements
Zone A 0 to 6 months Probation only, no confinement required.
Zone B 1 to 12 months Probation with intermittent confinement, community confinement, or home detention.
Zone C 10 to 18 months Split sentence: at least half of the minimum range served in prison, remainder in home/community confinement.
Zone D 12+ months Incarceration required for the full term (no probation options).

Federal BOP Security Level Points Thresholds

Security Level Male Points Threshold Female Points Threshold
Minimum (Camp) 0 to 11 points 0 to 15 points
Low Security 12 to 15 points 16 to 30 points
Medium Security 16 to 23 points 31+ points (High/Medium combined)
High Security 24+ points N/A (FCI / Camp placements)
